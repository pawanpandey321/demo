types of generations

first-gen-Computer

The generations of computers are typically divided into five distinct phases: First Generation (1940-1956): Vacuum Tubes. Second Generation (1956-1963): Transistors. Third Generation (1964-1971): Integrated Circuits. Fourth Generation (1971-Present): Microprocessors

second-gen-Computer

Second-generation computers, spanning roughly from 1956 to 1963, are characterized by the use of transistors instead of vacuum tubes, which were used in the first generation. This shift led to smaller, faster, and more efficient computers. Additionally, magnetic core memory was introduced, offering faster and more reliable storage.

third-gen-Computer

Third-generation computers, spanning roughly from 1965 to 1971, were characterized by the use of integrated circuits (ICs), also known as microchips. This technology led to smaller, faster, more reliable, and less expensive computers compared to previous generations that relied on transistors. Key features included the development of higher-level programming languages, the introduction of operating systems, and the emergence of user-friendly interfaces like keyboards and mice.

forth-gen-Computer

Forth Generation Of Computers These computers used the VLSI technology or the Very Large Scale Integrated (VLSI) circuits technology. Therefore they were also known as the microprocessors. Intel was the first company to develop a microprocessor.
